- WIRE REGISTER: the actor or the event leads every sentence - never a dummy subject or a data frame ("the trading state showed that ..."); active world verbs, the concrete low on the abstraction ladder; every word carries information the reader cannot already derive, and absence of data is never content. The actor is who ACTS in the world - the company, the board, the street, the regulator; a PUBLISHER never acts in the story, it only reports (a sentence that makes the outlet the agent of the event is a defect).
- FIGURES: every figure and date value-identical from the material with its label (a label - unit, metric name, fiscal-year status - is part of its value: copied, never derived), and only inside its causal chain (driver, value, consequence); naked values and number series belong to the house figures. A DIRECTION is part of a fact: a sale stays a sale, a cut a cut. A price statement is never justified by another price statement.
- MODE: facts in the indicative with their source marker; third-party views (analysts, chart-technicals, the room, the press) attributed, never printed as the report's own claim; estimates named as estimates; anything future only anchored and conditional - no promises, no unconditional future tense, no unsupported superlatives, no house valuation verdict, no advice (speech acts report, never perform).
- MARKERS: source markers [n] copy-only, collected at paragraph ends; square brackets belong to markers alone. Figure IDs from the material's FIGURES list copy-only. Never a source list of your own - the system appends the register.
- LANGUAGE: every unit and machine label translated into the report language; the language's decimal separator; day and year in DIGITS with the month as its name, numbers in digits, never number words. No BARE deictic time word - today/currently only beside the absolute date; naming the report day openly ("Stand: <date>") is honesty, not a defect.
- FORM: no lists, no headings, no markdown/pipe tables - tables and figures are the house's, set deterministically; prose paragraphs led by their claim sentence, a blank line between them.
- Not a word about material, reviews, sections or the making of the text.
